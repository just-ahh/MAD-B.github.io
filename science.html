Glasgow Science Centre

Key Information

Tickets range from £11.50 - £14.75.

Add ons including the Planetarium, IMAX and Glasgow Tower cost seperately.

Open 10am to 5pm everyday from April to September. Open 10am to 3pm Wednesday to Thursday and 10am to 5pm on weekends November to March.

15 minutes walk from exhibition centre train station. Many bus links from city centre.

Has own parking for £3 day rate.

Autopsy Game

This game involves looking at different autopsy scans and looking at the different analysis of them to discover new information about each person.

The game uses real scans of people who donated them to science and is an interesting way to look at what information can be found out about a person after death.

Players need to use the touchscreen to pick different scan layers and use the tools at the side to look at cross sections of the body to see more information.

Bronchoscopy Game

This game involves the player using a joystick style controller to look at a patients lungs to navigate to the problem area.

Once at the problem area, the player has to look at the medical issues there (represented as coloured dots) and link them to a diagnosis.

Players can use both dexterity and problem solving to arrive at the correct diagnoses for as many patients as possible within the time limit.

Digging for oil

Players have to sit down in a chair to be able to control the game on screen, users are in the control of an oil digger and must control direction, pressure and rotation speed to be able to safely dig for oil.

The controls positioned on the armchair look simple but the act of multitasking all the different components is challenging.
